Classical vs. Quantum Physics

The Two Worlds of Physics

Classical Physics describes the world we see every day. Think of a thrown ball or a planet orbiting the sun. Its motion is predictable; if you know its position and velocity, you can calculate its exact path. It's deterministic and continuous.

Quantum Physics, on the other hand, governs the bizarre world of atoms and particles. Here, things are not so certain. A particle doesn't have a definite position until it's measured. Instead, it exists in a "probability wave" of possible locations. It's probabilistic and quantized, meaning energy and other properties come in discrete packets.
